Tales from my European Summer Abroad

This past summer I studied abroad in Valencia, Spain. It was a very enjoyable experience and I got to immerse myself in both a language and culture that I love. At times during my study abroad and for a month and a half afterwards I would take trips to other places and countries. Below you will find some of my favourite places that I visited and memories I created during my travels.

I have also included my Travel Schedule from this past summer in case you are curious.

Pompeii/Salerno

My sister Meredith met up with my friend Lauren and me for our Roman leg of the European tour. We spent most of our six days together in Rome, but decided to take a one day trip to Pompeii to check out all the ruins. We had to take a train to Salerno and from there we caught a bus to reach our destination – the small town of Pompeii.

When we got there we spent a few hours doing an audio tour of the ruins and then headed to dinner. However, we lost track of time at dinner and ended up having to run back to the bus stop to make our bus. Unfortunately, we ended up standing on the wrong side of the street and an Italian woman convinced us that we were waiting for the same bus as her. Perhaps the fact that we were only able to communicate with her by using the similarities between Spanish and Italian should have made us double check her advice (or what we thought was the advice she was giving), but that was not the case. Thus, we ended up watching our nonstop charter bus drive by and getting on a city bus to Salerno instead. Shortly after boarding the bus we realised that we would arrive that the train station a full half hour after the last train back to Rome had left the station. Luckily, a nice university student from Salerno overheard us and ended up helping us book our train tickets for the following day and finding us a hostel to stay in that night.

When we arrived at our hostel we had no clothes or luggage with us whatsoever aside from our wallets and phones, we were disgusting from having spent all day in the heat exploring dirty ruins and we were extremely tired. My sister Meredith and I ended up finding some laundry detergent in the community bathroom so we used that as soap to take crude showers just to get a little bit cleaner.

As if all that wasn't bad enough, by the time we finally got to bed we only had four hours to sleep before we had to wake up to catch our train back to Rome. While this was one of the worst travel errors I have ever made, I think it may be the funniest story I've ever gotten from my travels.

Munich

Munich was a great city to visit because it was very clean and very quaint. I don’t speak a word of German, so I really appreciated that nearly everyone I ran into in Germany spoke English in addition to German.

While in Munich my friends Lauren, Shawn, Jesse and I took a walking tour of the city, ate at several beer gardens, visited Dachau concentration camp and enjoyed the city’s extensive park, among other things. My favourite memory from my time in Munich must have been my discovery of a new, edgy sport – river surfing.

There is a fast-moving river that runs right through the centre of Munich’s scenic park and many people have used this river to practice hanging ten on some white-water rapids. Who’d have guessed landlocked Munich of all places would have so many surfer dudes!

This that I filmed while in Munich really captures how amazing the sport is. I think the most impressive part is how the surfers are able to just jump in and start surfing immediately after the previous surfer wipes out without even missing a beat!
